# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The project requires a review environment where technical disagreement is handled precisely, respectfully, and without personal attacks. Genealogy work often involves uncertainty, conflicting evidence, and culturally sensitive material; community conduct must support careful analysis rather than escalation.

## Scope

This policy applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, design reviews, and any project-managed communication space.

## Audience

- Contributors
- Reviewers
- Maintainers

## Dependencies

Community participation assumes compliance with the contribution process in [`/CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Related Documents

- Security escalation: [`/SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md)

## Expected Behavior

- Critique ideas, evidence handling, or architecture rather than people.
- State assumptions and uncertainty explicitly.
- Preserve attribution when extending another contributor's work.
- Be careful with claims involving living individuals, sensitive records, or ambiguous identity.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language
- Misrepresentation of sources or review outcomes
- Dismissive treatment of domain expertise, especially from archivists and genealogists
- Repeatedly bypassing project standards after review feedback

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove content that violates this policy and may limit participation for repeated or severe violations. Enforcement decisions should document the behavior, the violated expectation, and the corrective action.
